Year 7 Digital Technologies
Unit 1 — Digital Literacy
St Brendan's College (Yeppoon), 2026
Unit overview Cohort and/or class considerations
Start week: Term 1 Week 1
Finish week: Term 1 Week 10
In this unit, students develop foundational digital literacy skills by learning to confidently and responsibly use common digital
tools and systems. Through practical, hands-on activities, students explore Microsoft Word, PowerPoint and Excel to create,
manage and communicate information using appropriate conventions. The unit also introduces key concepts in cybersecurity,
digital footprints and emerging technologies such as artificial intelligence, helping students understand how data is created,
shared and protected in networked environments. Across the term, students build skills in selecting suitable digital tools,
managing files and data, and using technology safely and ethically, preparing them for further learning in Digital Technologies
and other learning areas.
Assessment 1
Year 7
Title Digital Tasks
Description Students will create a digital portfolio that demonstrates their learning across the term through a collection of completed tasks and reflections. The
portfolio includes evidence of using word processing, presentation and spreadsheet tools to create, organise, analyse and communicate information,
as well as activities focused on cybersecurity awareness, safe online behaviour and responsible use of AI tools. Students are expected to select and
use digital tools efficiently, apply common conventions, manage data accurately, and demonstrate an understanding of how information is shared and
secured online. The portfolio provides ongoing evidence of skill development, responsible digital practices and the ability to reflect on learning progress
over time, aligned to the Year 7–8 Digital Technologies standards.

Achievement
standard
By the end of Year 8 students develop and modify creative digital solutions, decompose real-world problems, and evaluate alternative solutions against
user stories and design criteria. Students acquire, interpret and model data with spreadsheets and represent data with integers and binary. They design
and trace algorithms and implement them in a general-purpose programming language. Students select appropriate hardware for particular tasks,
explain how data is transmitted and secured in networks, and identify cyber security threats. They select and use a range of digital tools efficiently and
responsibly to create, locate and share content; and to plan, collaborate on and manage projects. Students manage their digital footprint.
Moderation Monitoring: Week 7
Conferencing: Week 9
