Band 9–10 Design and Technologies 2026
Curriculum and assessment plan
St Brendan's College (Yeppoon)
Level description Context and cohort considerations
By the end of Year 10 students should have had the opportunity to design and produce at least 4 designed solutions focused on one or more of the 4 technologies contexts:
• Engineering principles and systems
• Food and fibre production
• Food specialisations
• Materials and technologies specialisations.
Students should have opportunities to experience creating designed solutions for products, services and environments.
Students use design and technologies knowledge and understanding, processes and production skills and design thinking to produce designed solutions for identified needs or opportunities
of relevance to individuals and local, regional or global communities. They work independently and collaboratively. Problem-solving activities acknowledge the complexities of contemporary
life and make connections to related specialised occupations and further study. Increasingly, study has a global perspective, with opportunities to understand the complex interdependencies
involved in the development of technologies and enterprises.
Students specifically focus on preferred futures, taking into account ethics; legal issues; social values; and economic, environmental and social sustainability factors; and use strategies such
as life cycle thinking. They use critical thinking, creativity, innovation and enterprise skills with increasing confidence, independence and collaboration. Students analyse data, evaluate design
ideas and technologies, respond to feedback, and evaluate design processes used to inform designed solutions for preferred futures.
Using a range of technologies including a variety of graphical representation techniques to communicate, students generate and represent original ideas and production plans in 2-
dimensional and 3-dimensional representations. These techniques will be specific to the technologies context and may include scale, perspective, orthogonal and production drawings with
sectional and exploded views. Students produce rendered, illustrated views for marketing and use graphic visualisation software to produce dynamic views of design ideas and designed
solutions.
Students identify the steps involved in planning the production of designed solutions. They develop detailed project management plans, incorporating elements such as sequenced time, cost
and action plans, to manage design tasks safely. Students apply management plans, making adjustments when necessary, to successfully complete design tasks. They identify and establish
safety procedures that minimise risk and manage projects with safety and efficiency in mind, maintaining safety standards and management procedures to ensure success.

Duration 1 Semester 1 Semester 1 Semester 1 Term
Description In this unit, students investigate the engineering
principles behind bridge design, with a focus on
how forces, materials, and structural systems
interact to create safe and effective structures.
Students explore tension, compression, load
distribution, and stability, and examine how material
properties influence strength, durability, and
sustainability. Through analysis of real-world
bridges and hands-on design tasks, students
develop an understanding of how engineers
consider technical, environmental, and practical
factors when designing engineered solutions. The
unit builds students’ knowledge of materials and
forces while developing their ability to apply the
design process to solve authentic engineering
problems.
In this unit, students explore sustainable living
through the lens of resilience and self-sufficiency,
examining how designed solutions can support
survival and wellbeing in extreme or off-grid
scenarios. Students investigate sustainability from
ecological, economic, and social perspectives,
considering how ethical decision-making,
resource management, and long-term impacts
influence design choices. Through analysis of
real-world sustainable systems such as energy,
water, food production, and shelter, students
develop an understanding of how designers
and engineers respond to challenges related to
security, sustainability, and human needs. The
unit emphasises thoughtful planning, innovation,
and the responsible use of technologies to create
sustainable living environments.
In this unit, students explore commercial design
by examining how products are developed to meet
user needs and succeed in competitive markets.
Students investigate market analysis, target
audiences, product design and manufacturing
processes, and basic business principles such
as value proposition, cost, and sustainability.
Through analysis of existing commercial products
and innovation case studies, students develop
an understanding of how designers balance
functionality, aesthetics, ethical considerations,
and commercial viability. The unit emphasises
innovation, enterprise skills, and informed decision-
making in the development of market-ready
designed solutions.
In this unit, students explore food design and
preparation through the use of a virtual kitchen
environment. They investigate how food trends
have evolved over time, comparing historical food
practices with contemporary and emerging trends
influenced by culture, technology, health, and
sustainability. Students examine how sensory
and functional properties of food affect design
decisions, including flavour, texture, presentation,
and nutritional value. By working in a virtual cooking
space, students develop an understanding of how
technologies can be used to design, test, and
communicate food solutions in innovative and
safe ways, while considering social and cultural
influences on food choices.

Description In this assessment, students complete an
examination that assesses their understanding
of the engineering concepts underpinning bridge
design. The exam includes questions on forces
such as tension, compression, and load distribution,
as well as the characteristics and properties of
materials used in bridge construction. Students also
explain the factors engineers must consider when
designing bridges, including safety, sustainability,
materials selection, and intended use. This
assessment focuses on students’ ability to analyse
engineering principles and apply theoretical
knowledge to real-world design contexts.
In this assessment, students design a sustainable
doomsday shelter in response to a given scenario
that requires living off the grid for an extended
period of time. Students analyse the needs and
constraints of the scenario, develop a design
brief, and investigate systems and resources
related to shelter, energy, water, food, and waste
management. Using SketchUp, students create
and refine a digital model of their shelter, justifying
design decisions with reference to ecological,
economic, and social sustainability considerations.
Students evaluate their design against developed
criteria, including sustainability and practicality, and
communicate their solution using digital tools while
demonstrating effective project planning and safe,
appropriate use of technologies.
In this assessment, students complete an
examination that assesses their understanding
of key commercial design principles. The exam
includes questions on how designers analyse
needs and opportunities, consider ethical and
sustainability factors, and apply innovation and
enterprise skills when developing commercial
products. Students demonstrate knowledge
of materials, systems, and manufacturing
considerations, as well as basic business concepts
such as target audiences, market needs, and value
creation. This assessment focuses on students’
ability to explain and apply foundational commercial
design knowledge to authentic product design
contexts.
In this assessment, students analyse historical
and modern food trends and select one trend as
the basis for a designed food solution. Students
develop a design brief and plan a dish that reflects
the chosen trend, considering sensory, functional,
and sustainability factors. Using a virtual kitchen,
students design and prepare their dish and
create a VR cooking video that demonstrates the
preparation process and final outcome. Students
justify their design decisions in relation to the
selected food trend and evaluate their solution
against developed criteria, communicating their
ideas and processes using digital technologies.

Assessment 2 — Paddle-pop stick bridges Assessment 4 — Sustainability Exam Assessment 6 — Shark Tank
Term 2 4 3
Week Week 9 Week 9 Week 5
Description In this collaborative design project, students work
in groups to apply the full design process to create
a paddle pop stick bridge in response to a given
design scenario. Students analyse the problem,
develop a design brief, research forces and
materials, generate and prototype design ideas,
and construct a bridge using appropriate tools and
techniques. They test and evaluate their bridge
against design criteria, refine their solution based
on testing results, and present their final design
and justification to an audience. This assessment
emphasises teamwork, project management,
safe production skills, and the ability to evaluate
and communicate engineered solutions using
engineering and sustainability considerations.
In this assessment, students complete an
examination that assesses their understanding of
key sustainability principles. The exam includes
questions on how designers analyse needs and
opportunities, consider ethical and sustainability
factors. Students demonstrate knowledge of
ecological, economical and societal sustainability
as well as sketching and representation techniques.
This assessment focuses on students’ ability
to explain and apply foundational sustainability
design knowledge across product, service and/or
environmental design contexts.
In this assessment, students respond to a given
design situation by developing a commercial
product for a specified target audience. Students
follow the design process to analyse user needs
and market factors, define a design brief, generate
and prototype ideas, and evaluate and refine their
solution against developed criteria. They consider
materials, systems, manufacturing processes,
sustainability, and commercial viability when
making design decisions. Students communicate
their final designed solution through appropriate
representations and justify their decisions,
demonstrating innovation, enterprise skills, and
effective use of technologies throughout the design
process.

Unit 5 — Empathy
Year level 10
Duration 1 Term
Description In this unit, students develop an understanding of Australian and Aboriginal histories and cultures while building empathy as a core design skill. Students explore how culture, context, and lived experience influence
design decisions, and examine the importance of ethical and respectful design when responding to Aboriginal perspectives. Through guided learning, students consider how designers can create solutions that
acknowledge cultural significance, community needs, and social impact. The unit emphasises empathy, reflection, and responsible decision-making, encouraging students to design with awareness of Australia’s
cultural diversity and shared histories.
Assessment 8 — Human Centered Design
Term 4
Week Week 8
Description In this assessment, students complete a designed solution task under exam-style time constraints to simulate working to a real-world deadline. Students are given an Australia-based design scenario and follow the
final stages of the design process to generate, revise, evaluate, and refine a solution. Evaluation of the design must explicitly demonstrate empathy skills, including consideration of user perspectives, cultural context,
and social impact. This assessment focuses on students’ ability to apply ethical, culturally informed, and reflective thinking to design decisions.
