Year 8 Digital Technologies
Unit 3 — Cardbaord Arcade
St Brendan's College (Yeppoon), 2026
Unit overview Cohort and/or class considerations
Start week: Term 3 Week 1
Finish week: Term 4 Week 9
In this unit, students explore physical computing by learning to program Arduino microcontrollers and apply these skills to the
design and construction of interactive arcade machines. Students develop an understanding of algorithms, basic programming
structures, and how hardware specifications influence performance and functionality. The design process is emphasised
through the use of user stories and design criteria, guiding students to create solutions that meet specific user needs. Through
iterative design, testing and refinement, students combine computational thinking with creativity, problem-solving and hands-on
construction skills.

Assessment 4
Year 8
Title Hardware and Algorithms Exam
Description In this assessment, students complete a written exam that assesses their understanding of foundational algorithms and hardware selection in the context
of Arduino systems. Students demonstrate their ability to define and decompose problems, design and trace simple algorithms, and predict program
outputs. The exam also evaluates students’ understanding of how hardware specifications affect performance and how to select appropriate components
for specific tasks. This assessment provides evidence of students’ theoretical knowledge and computational thinking skills that underpin practical digital
solutions.
Technique Examination
Mode Written
Conditions • up to 50 minutes plus 10 minutes planning time
• under supervised conditions
• short response up to 75 words per item, up to 400 words for the task
• extended response 100–200 words per item, up to 400 words for the task
• 1–2 A3 pages or equivalent digital media that may include graphical representations with annotations
Timing Week 6
Achievement
standard
By the end of Year 8 students develop and modify creative digital solutions, decompose real-world problems, and evaluate alternative solutions against
user stories and design criteria. Students acquire, interpret and model data with spreadsheets and represent data with integers and binary. They design
and trace algorithms and implement them in a general-purpose programming language. Students select appropriate hardware for particular tasks,
explain how data is transmitted and secured in networks, and identify cyber security threats. They select and use a range of digital tools efficiently and
responsibly to create, locate and share content; and to plan, collaborate on and manage projects. Students manage their digital footprint.
Moderation Cross marking to be completed within the department

Assessment 5
Year 8
Title Arcade Machine
Description In this assessment, students design and build a functioning cardboard arcade machine based on a provided user profile and set of user stories. Students
apply the design process to define requirements, establish design criteria, and plan a solution that meets the needs of the target user. They design the
user experience, develop and test algorithms, and implement programs using Arduino hardware and sensors. Students iteratively refine their designs,
evaluate their solutions against the design criteria and user stories, and communicate their ideas using appropriate digital tools. The project demonstrates
students’ ability to integrate hardware selection, programming, creative design and project management to develop a complete digital solution.
Technique Project
Mode Multimodal
Conditions 2–4 A3 pages or equivalent digital media pages that may include annotated graphical representations
Timing Week 9
Achievement
standard
By the end of Year 8 students develop and modify creative digital solutions, decompose real-world problems, and evaluate alternative solutions against
user stories and design criteria. Students acquire, interpret and model data with spreadsheets and represent data with integers and binary. They design
and trace algorithms and implement them in a general-purpose programming language. Students select appropriate hardware for particular tasks,
explain how data is transmitted and secured in networks, and identify cyber security threats. They select and use a range of digital tools efficiently and
responsibly to create, locate and share content; and to plan, collaborate on and manage projects. Students manage their digital footprint.
Moderation Monitoring: Week 6
