Year 9 Digital Technologies
Unit 1 — Websites 101
St Brendan's College (Yeppoon), 2026
Unit overview Cohort and/or class considerations
Start week: Term 1 Week 1
Finish week: Term 1 Week 5
In this unit, students will explore the foundational technologies behind the
internet and how websites function. They will examine how digital systems use
hardware and software to manage and secure access to data across networks.
Through guided investigation and hands-on tasks, students will learn how
websites are constructed by separating content, structure, and presentation
using HTML and CSS. They will also be introduced to simple data compression
techniques and understand their importance in efficient web communication.
This unit builds the knowledge and skills required for designing and analysing
secure, well-structured, and efficient digital content online.
The Years 9–10 cohort consists of a diverse group of boys with a wide range of digital
competencies, from students with strong technical skills and personal interest in IT,
to those who require further support to confidently engage with digital systems. Many
students come from regional or rural backgrounds, where access to structured digital
learning opportunities may have been limited. The program is designed to build on
prior knowledge while introducing more advanced and applied skills relevant to future
study and employment. The curriculum supports the school’s priority to equip students
with transferable digital skills, critical thinking abilities, and an understanding of ethical
technology use in a rapidly changing world.

Assessment 1
Year 9
Title Web Exam
Description Students will complete a written exam that assesses their understanding of how websites function and how data is managed in networked digital systems.
The exam will include questions on the roles of hardware and software in securing and controlling access to data, the structure of web documents using
HTML and CSS, and basic data compression techniques.
Technique Examination
Mode Written
Conditions • up to 50 minutes plus 10 minutes planning time
• under supervised conditions
• short response up to 100 words per item, up to 600 words for the task
• extended response 200–300 words per item, up to 600 words for the task
Timing Week 5
Achievement
standard
By the end of Year 10 students develop and modify innovative digital solutions, decompose real-world problems, and critically evaluate alternative
solutions against stakeholder elicited user stories. Students acquire, interpret and model complex data with databases and represent documents
as content, structure and presentation. They design and validate algorithms and implement them, including in an object-oriented programming
language. Students explain how digital systems manage, control and secure access to data; and model cyber security threats and explore a
vulnerability. They use advanced features of digital tools to create interactive content, and to plan, collaborate on, and manage agile projects. Students
apply privacy principles to manage digital footprints.
Moderation A sample of student portfolios representing a range of achievement levels will be reviewed by another Digital Technologies teacher, who will provide
feedback on the consistency and fairness of the grading. Any necessary adjustments will be made to ensure alignment with the assessment criteria and
standards.

Key teaching and

Students are introduced to how web
content is structured using HTML. The
concept of representing information as
content and structure is explicitly taught.
Students learn about common HTML
elements (headings, paragraphs, images,
links) and the purpose of semantic
structure.
Students begin writing basic HTML
documents using a code editor. They
create a simple webpage including
headings, paragraphs, and images.
Emphasis is placed on correct syntax, file
structure, and readability of code.
Ongoing formative assessment through
teacher observation and feedback on
HTML tasks. Short-response questions

3 Key teaching and
learning experiences
Students explore the concept of
presentation using CSS and how it
separates design from structure. Key
concepts include selectors, properties,
and values, as well as the importance of
consistency and maintainability in web
design.
Students extend their existing HTML
pages by applying CSS styling. Tasks
include changing colours, fonts, spacing,
and layout. Students experiment with
linking external stylesheets and modifying
visual presentation without altering
content.
Formative assessment through completion
of a styled webpage task. Students
respond to short-answer questions
explaining the difference between HTML
and CSS and why separation is important.
Teacher provides feedback on both code
and explanations.

Key teaching and
learning experiences
Students investigate simple data
compression techniques and their
importance in web communication.
Concepts such as file size, load time,
and efficiency are explored. Students
examine examples of compressed vs
uncompressed data and discuss the
impact on user experience.
Students apply optimisation techniques
to their webpages, such as resizing
images and reducing file sizes. They
compare performance differences
between optimised and non-optimised
files. Activities may include using online
tools to compress images and analyse file
sizes.
Short-response and extended-response

data compression and its importance.
Retrieval practice covering all topics
to date (digital systems, HTML, CSS).

preparation for the exam.
