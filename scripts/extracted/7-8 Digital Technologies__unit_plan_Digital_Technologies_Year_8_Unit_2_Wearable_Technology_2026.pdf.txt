Year 8 Digital Technologies
Unit 2 — Wearable Technology
St Brendan's College (Yeppoon), 2026
Unit overview Cohort and/or class considerations
Start week: Term 1 Week 1
Finish week: Term 2 Week 10
In this unit, students use Micro:bit devices to explore data, communication and physical computing in a practical and age-
appropriate context. Students learn how Microbits collect, transmit and represent data, with a focus on radio communication,
binary representation and basic data security concepts. Through guided investigations, students explore how data can be
gathered from sensors, transmitted between devices and analysed to identify patterns and trends. In the second part of the unit,
students apply these skills to design and create wearable technologies, combining computational thinking, data analysis and
ethical considerations when collecting data about people.

Assessment 2
Year 8
Title Data Exam
Description In this assessment, students complete a written exam that assesses their understanding of how Microbits communicate and represent data. Students
explain the differences between broadcast and direct radio communication, and demonstrate their understanding of how text, numbers and messages
are represented using integers and binary. The exam also assesses students’ ability to model and reason about structured data, design and trace basic
algorithms, and predict program outputs related to Microbit communication and simple encryption. This assessment provides evidence of students’
conceptual understanding of data transmission, representation and algorithmic thinking.
Technique Examination
Mode Written
Conditions • up to 50 minutes plus 10 minutes planning time
• under supervised conditions
• short response up to 75 words per item, up to 400 words for the task
• extended response 100–200 words per item, up to 400 words for the task
Timing Week 7
Achievement
standard
By the end of Year 8 students develop and modify creative digital solutions, decompose real-world problems, and evaluate alternative solutions against
user stories and design criteria. Students acquire, interpret and model data with spreadsheets and represent data with integers and binary. They design
and trace algorithms and implement them in a general-purpose programming language. Students select appropriate hardware for particular tasks,
explain how data is transmitted and secured in networks, and identify cyber security threats. They select and use a range of digital tools efficiently and
responsibly to create, locate and share content; and to plan, collaborate on and manage projects. Students manage their digital footprint.
Moderation Cross marked within the department

Assessment 3
Year 8
Title Wearable Tech
Description In this assessment, students design and develop a wearable Microbit-based solution to collect and analyse data related to a given investigation task.
Students plan how data will be gathered using appropriate sensors, design algorithms to collect and store the data, and use digital tools to analyse and
visualise results to draw conclusions. They select suitable hardware, design the user experience, manage the project process and evaluate their solution
against design criteria and user needs. Students also demonstrate responsible and ethical data collection practices, reflecting on the purpose and impact
of their digital solution.
Technique Investigation
Mode Multimodal
Conditions 2–4 A3 pages or equivalent digital media pages that may include annotated graphical representations
